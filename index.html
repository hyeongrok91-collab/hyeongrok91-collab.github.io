The Information Theory of AI Architectures: Effective Computational Capacity and Intelligence Decomposition
Hyeong Rok Lee · March 2026 · Preprint
Abstract

We define Effective Computational Capacity (ECC) as the generic Jacobian rank of the realization map from parameters to functions. This quantity measures the number of functionally independent parameter directions of a neural architecture, and we prove that it is the unique capacity measure satisfying three natural axioms: equivalence-invariance, direct-sum additivity, and normalization. We compute corrected ECC formulas for standard transformer components. For full multi-head attention with H heads, head dimension d_h, and value dimension d_v, ECC_Attn = H[d_h(2d−d_h) + d_v(2d−d_v)]. For a width-m ReLU feed-forward network, ECC_FFN = m(2d−1). We define task intelligence by Intelligence(T) = ECC · τ_rep · τ_stat · τ_opt, where τ_rep is representational alignment, τ_stat statistical efficiency, and τ_opt optimization quality. We prove a conditional coding theorem linking excess risk to an aligned approximation dimension k, and we isolate the exact point at which a purely static bridge from ECC to performance fails. The correct local bridge is a measurable residual-tail quantity η_T(k) derived from the pullback task metric M_T = J*ᵀ G_T J*.

Paper A — Foundation
The d² Pullback Theorem: Why Attention is a d²-Dimensional Problem
Hyeong Rok Lee · March 2026 · Preprint
Abstract

Self-attention is usually described as an n × n operation. We argue that this is the wrong intrinsic dimension. For a single head with token matrix X ∈ ℝⁿˣᵈ and parameter product M = W_Q W_Kᵀ ∈ ℝᵈˣᵈ, the score matrix has the constrained bilinear form S(M) = XMXᵀ/√d. Our first result is the d² pullback theorem: for any differentiable loss through a row-wise attention map, the gradient with respect to M is exactly the sandwich transform X⊤G(M)X/√d. Hence optimization depends only on the bilinear score subspace whose dimension is rank(X)² and equals d² whenever X has full column rank. Our second result computes the exact local Jacobian rank for analytic row normalizers: rank D_M P(0) = r(r − ε_X). We further prove same-depth uniform replacement of softmax attention by positive polynomial attention, formulate a conditional depth-compensation theorem, and establish a no-go theorem for universal fixed-width degree-2 simulation.
