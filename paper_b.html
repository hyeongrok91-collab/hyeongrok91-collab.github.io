The Information Theory of AI Architectures: Effective Computational Capacity and Intelligence Decomposition

Hyeong Rok Lee · March 2026 · Preprint
📄 PDF 🔗 DOI

Abstract

We define Effective Computational Capacity (ECC) as the generic Jacobian rank of the realization map from parameters to functions. This quantity measures the number of functionally independent parameter directions of a neural architecture, and we prove that it is the unique capacity measure satisfying three natural axioms: equivalence-invariance, direct-sum additivity, and normalization. We compute corrected ECC formulas for standard transformer components. For full multi-head attention with $H$ heads, head dimension $d_h$, and value dimension $d_v$,

$$\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{Attn}} = H\big[d_h(2d - d_h) + d_v(2d - d_v)\big].$$

For a width-$m$ ReLU feed-forward network,

$$\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{FFN}} = m(2d - 1).$$

We define task intelligence by

$$\mathrm{Intelligence}(T) = \mathrm{ECC} \cdot \tau_{\mathrm{rep}} \cdot \tau_{\mathrm{stat}} \cdot \tau_{\mathrm{opt}},$$

where $\tau_{\mathrm{rep}}$ is representational alignment, $\tau_{\mathrm{stat}}$ statistical efficiency, and $\tau_{\mathrm{opt}}$ optimization quality. The operational identity

$$\tau_{\mathrm{rep}} = \eta_{\mathrm{eff}} = \frac{k_{\mathrm{use}}}{\mathrm{ECC}}.$$

shows that $\mathrm{ECC}\tau_{\mathrm{rep}}$ is the usable task-aligned dimension. We then prove a conditional coding theorem linking excess risk to an aligned approximation dimension $k$, and we isolate the exact point at which a purely static bridge from ECC to performance fails. The correct local bridge is a measurable residual-tail quantity $\eta_T(k)$ derived from the pullback task metric $M_T = J_*^\top G_T J_*$. At a larger scale, performance obeys a six-term decomposition separating existence, geometry, slice selection, reachability, generalization, and optimization.

Applications include a full ECC calculation for transformer attention including value and output maps, a function-space derivation of FFN capacity, a proof that standard Mamba blocks have $\mathrm{ECC} \approx \tfrac{1599}{256}d^2 \approx 6.246\,d^2$, a geometric account of LoRA and catastrophic forgetting, a workload-aware compression and data-curation principle, a formal coverage-efficiency split for universal versus specialized models, and a no-universal-$\tau_{\mathrm{rep}}$ theorem.

1. Introduction

Parameter count is the standard proxy for neural capacity, but it is a poor one. It does not distinguish active from inactive directions, it does not explain why different architectures with similar parameter counts behave differently, and it offers little guidance about why scaling laws, low-rank adaptation, sparse attention, and catastrophic forgetting often admit a common geometric structure. More fundamentally, it does not provide a mathematical definition of intelligence.

This paper proposes such a definition. The central object is the realization map $\Psi : \Theta \to \mathcal{F}$, which sends a parameter vector to the function computed by the architecture on its input domain. We measure the functional size of an architecture not by $\dim \Theta$ but by the generic rank of $D\Psi$. This quantity, which we call Effective Computational Capacity, counts the number of functionally independent parameter directions. It removes gauge symmetries, dead directions, and other forms of overparameterized redundancy by construction.

The paper builds directly on [1] The d² Pullback Theorem, which identified the $d^2$ score-side ceiling of bilinear attention and showed that degree-2 attention is Pareto-optimal on the score side. The present work extends that program in two directions.

Main contributions

  1. We prove that $\mathrm{ECC}(\Psi)=\mathrm{rank}_{\mathrm{gen}}(D\Psi)$ is the unique capacity measure satisfying equivalence-invariance, additivity, and normalization.
  2. We derive corrected formulas for full multi-head attention and ReLU feed-forward layers: $$\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{Attn}} = H\big[d_h(2d-d_h)+d_v(2d-d_v)\big], \quad \mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{FFN}} = m(2d-1).$$
  3. We resolve the multi-layer additivity question: the exact formula is $\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{total}}=\dim(\mathcal{S}_1+\cdots+\mathcal{S}_L)$, where $\mathcal{S}_l$ is the transported tangent image of layer $l$.
  4. We prove a conditional coding theorem and isolate a measurable local bridge to performance via the pullback task metric and its residual tail $\eta_T(k)$.
  5. We define task intelligence as capacity times utilization, with an operational representational factor $\tau_{\mathrm{rep}}=k_{\mathrm{use}}/\mathrm{ECC}$.
  6. We derive applications to LoRA, forgetting, self-training, architecture screening, workload-aware compression, data curation, emergence, and the technical tension between "scaling works" and "scaling wastes structure."
  7. We prove that no single architecture can maximize expected representational efficiency across all domain mixtures once mismatch crossovers exist.

2. Preliminaries

Throughout, $d$ denotes the model width, $H$ the number of attention heads, $d_h=d/H$ the head dimension, $d_v$ the value dimension, $m$ the hidden width of a feed-forward network, $n$ sequence length, and $L$ the number of layers. We write $\Theta \subseteq \mathbb{R}^P$ for parameter space, $\mathcal{F}$ for a function space over a compact input domain $\mathcal{X}$, and $\Psi:\Theta\to\mathcal{F}$ for the realization map.

For transformers we use the standard forms

$$S(X;M) = \mathrm{softmax}\!\left(\frac{XMX^\top}{\sqrt{d_h}}\right), \quad \mathrm{Attn}(X) = S(X;M)\, XV,$$

for a single head, where $M$ denotes the score kernel and $V$ a value map.

3. Effective Computational Capacity

3.1 Definition

Definition (Architecture-level ECC). The effective computational capacity of the architecture is the generic rank $$\boxed{\mathrm{ECC}(\Psi) := \mathrm{rank}_{\mathrm{gen}}(D\Psi) = \max_{\theta \in \Theta} \mathrm{rank}(D\Psi_\theta).}$$ For real-analytic realization maps, the maximum is attained on an open dense subset of $\Theta$.

3.2 Axiomatic Uniqueness

Theorem (Uniqueness of ECC). Let $C$ assign a nonneg. real number to every smooth realization map $\Psi:\Theta\to\mathcal{F}$. Assume:
  1. (A1) Equivalence-invariance. If $\Psi_1$ and $\Psi_2$ realize the same local family of functions, then $C(\Psi_1)=C(\Psi_2)$.
  2. (A2) Additivity. If $\Psi=\Psi_1\oplus\Psi_2$ is the direct sum of independent components, then $C(\Psi)=C(\Psi_1)+C(\Psi_2)$.
  3. (A3) Normalization. For the identity map $\mathrm{id}:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$, one has $C(\mathrm{id})=1$.
Then $C(\Psi) = \mathrm{rank}_{\mathrm{gen}}(D\Psi) = \mathrm{ECC}(\Psi)$ for every smooth realization map.
Proof. By equivalence-invariance, $C$ depends only on the locally realized function family. On any generic chart where the rank is constant, the constant-rank theorem identifies the image with a smooth manifold of dimension $r = \mathrm{rank}_{\mathrm{gen}}(D\Psi)$. Hence there exists $\varphi : \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}$ with $C(\Psi) = \varphi(r)$. Additivity gives $\varphi(r_1 + r_2) = \varphi(r_1) + \varphi(r_2)$ (Cauchy's equation on $\mathbb{N}$). Normalization gives $\varphi(1) = 1$, hence $\varphi(r) = r$. ∎
Remark. None of the axioms mentions Jacobian rank, so rank appears as a theorem rather than an assumption. This is structurally parallel to classical uniqueness theorems for entropy.

4. ECC of Transformer Blocks and Related Architectures

4.1 Single-head score-side capacity

Theorem (Single-head score-side ECC). Let $\Phi(W_Q,W_K) = W_Q W_K^\top$ with $W_Q, W_K \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d_h}$. Then $$\boxed{\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{score}} = d_h(2d - d_h).}$$ In the square case $d_h = d$, this reduces to $\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{score}} = d^2$.

4.2 Full multi-head attention

Theorem (Full MHA ECC). Consider multi-head attention with $H$ heads evaluated on input sequences of length $n \ge 2$. Then: $$\boxed{\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{Attn}} = H\big[d_h(2d-d_h) + d_v(2d-d_v)\big].}$$ Under the standard partition $d = Hd_h = Hd_v$: $$\boxed{\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{Attn}} = 4d^2 - \frac{2d^2}{H}.}$$

4.3 ReLU feed-forward networks

Theorem (FFN ECC). Consider a one-hidden-layer ReLU network $f(x) = W_2 \sigma(W_1 x)$ with $d \ge 2$. Then: $$\boxed{\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{FFN}} = m(2d-1).}$$ For the standard transformer choice $m = 4d$: $$\boxed{\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{FFN}} = 8d^2 - 4d.}$$
Proof sketch. Each hidden neuron has a one-dimensional positive-scaling gauge (ReLU homogeneity), giving $m$ kernel directions. In function space, distributional jumps across activation hyperplanes isolate each neuron separately, forcing the generic kernel to be exactly $m$-dimensional: $\mathrm{ECC} = 2md - m = m(2d-1)$. ∎

4.4 Multi-layer composition

Proposition (General composition formula). At any regular parameter point $\theta$: $$\boxed{\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{total}}(\theta) = \dim\big(\mathcal{S}_1(\theta) + \cdots + \mathcal{S}_L(\theta)\big).}$$

Exact additivity ($= \sum \mathrm{ECC}_l$) holds if and only if the transported tangent spaces form a direct sum. Three counterexamples (smooth linear, ReLU residual, attention at $T=1$) show that additivity failure is a fundamental phenomenon, not a pathology.

4.5 Mamba and selective state-space models

Theorem (Mamba ECC). For a standard Mamba-style block with defaults $m=2d$, $N=16$, $k=4$, $r=\lceil d/16\rceil$: $$\boxed{\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{Mamba}} = \tfrac{1599}{256}\,d^2 + O(d) \approx 6.246\,d^2 + O(d).}$$

4.6 Architectural comparison

5. Intelligence as Capacity Times Utilization

5.1 Master equation

Definition (Task intelligence). $$\boxed{\mathrm{Intelligence}(T) = \mathrm{ECC} \cdot \bar\tau_\Sigma, \qquad \bar\tau_\Sigma := \tau_{\mathrm{rep}} \tau_{\mathrm{stat}} \tau_{\mathrm{opt}}.}$$

This is a functional, task-relative definition: how much of the architecture's ceiling is aligned to the task, statistically identified, and actually realized by optimization.

5.2 Utilization factorization

Proposition. Let $k_{\mathrm{use}}$ = usable task-aligned dimension, $k_{\mathrm{obs}}$ = portion identifiable from data, $k_{\mathrm{reached}}$ = portion reached by optimizer. Define: $$\tau_{\mathrm{rep}} := \frac{k_{\mathrm{use}}}{\mathrm{ECC}}, \quad \tau_{\mathrm{stat}} := \frac{k_{\mathrm{obs}}}{k_{\mathrm{use}}}, \quad \tau_{\mathrm{opt}} := \frac{k_{\mathrm{reached}}}{k_{\mathrm{obs}}}.$$ Then $\bar\tau_\Sigma = k_{\mathrm{reached}} / \mathrm{ECC}$.

5.3 The pullback task metric

The canonical local object is the pullback task metric:

$$M_T(\theta^*) = J_*^\top G_T J_* = \Psi^* g_T$$

Its effective rank gives the operational $k_{\mathrm{use}}$, and $\tau_{\mathrm{rep}} = k_{\mathrm{use}} / \mathrm{ECC}$ is the representational efficiency — the fraction of architectural capacity genuinely usable for the task.

5.4 Practical levers

Intervention Primary effect Interpretation

Architecture redesign $\tau_{\mathrm{rep}} \uparrow$ improves task alignment

6. Conditional Coding Theorem and the Bridge Problem

6.1 Conditional coding theorem

Theorem (Conditional coding theorem). Under standard smoothness and covering-number assumptions, the learned predictor obeys: $$\mathbb{E}\|\hat f_{k,n} - f^*\|_{L^2}^2 \;\lesssim\; C_1 k^{-2\beta/d_{\mathrm{data}}} + C_2 \frac{k \log n}{n} + \delta_{\mathrm{opt}}.$$

6.2 Why a purely static bridge fails

Four counterexamples show that ECC alone cannot predict approximation quality:

  1. Rank does not imply approximation. An analytic full-rank polynomial family $\Psi(\theta)(x) = \sum \theta_j x^j$ has full Jacobian rank $P$, but cannot approximate the constant function $1$ ($\Psi(\theta)(0) = 0$ always).
  2. Global universality ≠ local aligned approximation. Universal approximation is a global existence statement; the bridge requires good approximants in a local aligned slice.
  3. $M_T$-optimal slices need not be approximation-optimal. The variance-maximizing eigenspace may differ from the bias-reducing direction.
  4. Dormant-neuron trap. A neuron with tiny outer weight $a_i \approx 0$ is invisible to $M_T$ but may contain crucial inner-weight structure.

6.3 The correct local bridge: residual tails

Definition (Pullback task metric). $$\boxed{M_T(\theta^*) := J_*^\top G_T J_*.}$$
Definition (Aligned residual tail). $$\eta_T(k) := \inf_{\delta \in V_{T,k},\; \|\delta\| \le r_k} \|e_T - J_* \delta\|_{G_T}.$$
Theorem (Local aligned approximation bridge). The aligned local approximation error satisfies: $$\frac{1}{2}\Big(\eta_T(k) - \frac{1}{2}M_2 r_k^2\Big)_+^2 \le A_T^{\mathrm{loc}}(k) \le \frac{1}{2}\Big(\eta_T(k) + \frac{1}{2}M_2 r_k^2\Big)^2.$$

6.4 Six-term decomposition

$$\mathcal{U}_T(k) = \beta_{\mathrm{exist}}(k) + \Gamma_{\mathrm{curv}}(k) + \Gamma_{\mathrm{sel}}(k) + \Gamma_{\mathrm{act}}(B,k) + C_{\mathrm{gen}}\frac{k}{n} + \delta_{\mathrm{opt}}$$

The six terms separate existence, geometry, selection, reachability, generalization, and optimization errors. ECC bounds the ambient range of $k$ ($k \le \mathrm{ECC}$) — it is a resource ceiling, analogous to channel capacity in information theory.

7. Framework in Action

7.1 Capacity geometry explains architectural phenomena

  • Bilinear attention is Pareto-optimal on the score side among degree-≤2 score families.
  • Sparse attention: when $n \gg d$, most pairwise token relations cannot be independently controlled ($d_h^2 / n^2 \approx 10^{-6}$ for $d_h=128$, $n=131072$).
  • LoRA as low-dimensional geometry: a rank-$r$ adapter on an $m \times n$ matrix accesses $r(m+n-r)$ directions, not $r$ directions.
  • Catastrophic forgetting is governed by subspace geometry ($\dim(U_1 + U_2)$, principal angles, optimization conflict), not just the scalar sum of task dimensions.

7.2 Intelligence decomposition explains training dynamics

  • Self-training is a fixed point: by the data-processing inequality, $I(D_{\mathrm{syn}}; Y^*) \le I(D_{\mathrm{orig}}; Y^*)$, so self-MLE cannot improve $\tau_{\mathrm{stat}}$.
  • Architecture screening: compare candidates by $(\mathrm{ECC}, \mathrm{ECC}/\mathrm{FLOP}, \text{expected } \tau_{\mathrm{rep}})$ before training.
  • Emergence as threshold crossing: smooth growth in $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ produces abrupt accuracy jumps when $k_{\mathrm{eff}} \gtrsim k(T)$.
  • Workload-aware compression: preserve the leading eigenspace of $M_W = \sum_i w_i M_{T_i}$.

8. Future Directions in Architectural Design

Complex-valued and quaternionic attention

For an unconstrained complex bilinear score kernel on a $d_h$-dimensional complex head space:

$$\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathbb{C}} = 2 d_h^2$$

More generally, $\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathbb{A}} = (\dim_\mathbb{R} \mathbb{A}) \cdot d_h^2$ for $\mathbb{A} \in \{\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{C}, \mathbb{H}\}$.

No universal representational optimum

Theorem (No universal τ_rep optimum). If two architectures $A_1, A_2$ exchange their efficiency ordering across two domains $D_1, D_2$ (each wins on its matched domain), then no single architecture maximizes expected representational efficiency for every probability distribution on $\{D_1, D_2\}$.

9. Numerical Illustrations

Three small-scale experiments verify that the mathematical objects ($\eta_T(k)$, eigenvalue spectra, aligned slices) are computable and carry empirically detectable signal:

  1. α-crossover validation: In teacher-student (digits, MLP), prediction accuracy 43/60 = 71.7% ($p = 5.33 \times 10^{-4}$). On GPT-2 (layer-11 attention, ~590K params), accuracy 7/7 = 100% ($p = 0.0078$).
  2. GPT-2 subspace intervention: $|c_i|^2$-guided selector wins 6/8 domains. Spearman correlation between energy gap and improvement: $r_s = 0.786$, $p = 0.021$.
  3. Local bridge bound verification: $\eta_T(k)$ achieves 0/40 bound violations with mean prediction error < 0.25% in both $G_T = I$ and $G_T \neq I$ settings.

10. Related Work and Discussion

Does scaling alone imply AGI?

Scaling raises ECC, so it genuinely raises the ceiling. But if $\tau_{\mathrm{rep}}, \tau_{\mathrm{stat}}, \tau_{\mathrm{opt}}$ remain bounded away from one, gains can be slow. The equation $\mathrm{ECC}_{\mathrm{req}}(I^*) = I^* / (\tau_{\mathrm{rep}} \tau_{\mathrm{stat}} \tau_{\mathrm{opt}})$ shows poor utilization forces much larger capacity.

Are large language models just parrots?

The framework turns a binary debate into a spectrum. High coverage with low efficiency = "parroting." High efficiency = capacity aligned with latent task structure. Most systems lie between these extremes.

Transformer versus Mamba

Transformers can have larger single-block ECC, while Mamba is stronger in ECC-per-compute on long sequences. The right question is which task distribution benefits more from random access vs. streaming.

11. Conclusion

This paper develops an information-theoretic view of AI architecture in which the central capacity object is

$$\mathrm{ECC}(\Psi) = \mathrm{rank}_{\mathrm{gen}}(D\Psi).$$

The quantity is the unique invariant satisfying equivalence-invariance, additivity, and normalization. It counts functional freedom rather than storage size. Task performance is governed by

$$\mathrm{Intelligence}(T) = \mathrm{ECC} \cdot \tau_{\mathrm{rep}} \tau_{\mathrm{stat}} \tau_{\mathrm{opt}}.$$

The product $\mathrm{ECC} \cdot \tau_{\mathrm{rep}}$ is exactly the usable task-aligned dimension. The pullback task metric $M_T = J_*^\top G_T J_*$ is the canonical local object, and its residual $\eta_T(k)$ provides a measurable local bridge from geometry to approximation error. Beyond the local regime, a six-term decomposition separates existence, geometry, selection, reachability, generalization, and optimization.

The slogan: Paper A defined the ceiling. This paper makes precise how much of the ceiling is used, why the unused part matters, and why alignment, not raw block capacity, is the deeper bottleneck.
