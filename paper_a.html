The d² Pullback Theorem: Why Attention is a d²-Dimensional Problem

Hyeong Rok Lee · March 2026 · Preprint
📄 PDF 🔗 DOI

Abstract

Self-attention is usually described as an n × n operation. We argue that this is the wrong intrinsic dimension. For a single head with token matrix X ∈ ℝⁿˣᵈ and parameter product M = W_Q W_Kᵀ ∈ ℝᵈˣᵈ, the score matrix has the constrained bilinear form S(M) = XMXᵀ/√d. Our first result is the d² pullback theorem: for any differentiable loss through a row-wise attention map, the gradient with respect to M is exactly the sandwich transform X⊤G(M)X/√d. Hence optimization depends only on the bilinear score subspace whose dimension is rank(X)² and equals d² whenever X has full column rank. Our second result computes the exact local Jacobian rank for analytic row normalizers: rank D_M P(0) = r(r − ε_X). We further prove same-depth uniform replacement of softmax attention by positive polynomial attention, formulate a conditional depth-compensation theorem, and establish a no-go theorem for universal fixed-width degree-2 simulation.
